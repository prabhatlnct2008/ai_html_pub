# Business Playbook Examples

Use these as structural examples for generating business-specific RAG playbooks.

## EXAMPLE_01

BUSINESS_TYPE: LOCAL SERVICE BUSINESS

BUSINESS_NAME: SKY PET GROOMING SERVICES

BUSINESS_PROFILE

Business Name: Sky Pet Grooming Services
Business Type: Local service business
Core Category: At-home pet grooming
Primary Geography: Bangalore
Primary Offer Type: Mobile dog and cat grooming at customer homes
Business Maturity Assumption: Small but professional local business seeking bookings through website leads

Business Summary:
Sky Pet Grooming Services is a mobile grooming business serving pet owners in Bangalore. Instead of asking owners to transport pets to a salon, the service comes to the customer’s home. The offer is especially attractive for busy urban pet owners, apartment residents, owners of anxious pets, and customers who value convenience and lower stress for their animals.

OFFER_STRUCTURE

Primary Services:
- Full grooming for dogs
- Full grooming for cats
- Bath and blow-dry
- Nail trimming
- Hygiene trimming
- Deshedding / coat maintenance
- Basic grooming packages by pet type or breed size

Offer Characteristics:
- Service is easy to understand
- Trust matters, but not at the same level as medical or legal services
- Convenience is a major selling point
- Service area matters because the business is local
- Booking friction should be low
- Strong visual design helps, but the website does not need a large portfolio to convert

Most Important Selling Angles:
- Grooming at home
- Less travel stress for pet and owner
- Better for anxious pets
- Easy scheduling for busy people
- Clean, convenient, pet-friendly service

TARGET_AUDIENCE

Primary Audience: Busy urban pet owners in Bangalore

Secondary Audience:
- Families with dogs or cats
- Working professionals in apartments
- Owners of pets that dislike travel or grooming salons
- Customers seeking routine pet hygiene and convenience

Audience Motivations:
- Save time
- Avoid transporting pet
- Reduce pet stress
- Get predictable grooming help
- Access a cleaner, more convenient service experience

Likely Questions Before Booking:
- What exactly is included?
- Do you groom both dogs and cats?
- Do you serve my area?
- Is this safe for anxious pets?
- How does the home visit process work?
- How do I book?
- What are the charges?

BUYER_BEHAVIOR

Buying Journey Type: Fast local service decision with light trust evaluation

User Intent Pattern:
The buyer often lands on the site wanting quick reassurance, not deep research. They want to know:
- Is this service relevant to me?
- Is it available in my area?
- Does it sound safe and convenient?
- What is the process?
- How do I book?

What Increases Conversion:
- Clear hero message
- Immediate mention of Bangalore / home service
- Fast explanation of service
- Early trust cues
- Service area confirmation
- Simple booking CTA
- FAQ for hesitation

What Increases Bounce:
- Vague headline
- Overly decorative homepage that hides the offer
- No service area clarity
- No explanation of how home grooming works
- Too much generic fluff
- Weak CTA placement

SITE_ARCHITECTURE_OPTION_A

ARCHITECTURE_TYPE: SINGLE_PAGE_SITE

When this is the right choice:
- the business is early-stage or lean
- the service is straightforward
- the main conversion goal is inquiry or booking
- there are not many service categories
- local SEO depth is not yet the top priority
- speed of launch matters more than content depth

PAGE_STRUCTURE

Page Count: 1

Page: Home

Suggested Section Order:
- Hero
- Trust bar
- Services
- Benefits
- How it works
- Testimonials
- Service area
- FAQ
- CTA band
- Contact
- Footer

WHY_THIS_ARCHITECTURE_WORKS

This architecture works because the buyer’s intent is simple and local. The homepage can answer nearly all high-priority questions in one scroll:
- what the service is
- why it is better than salon travel
- what is included
- how booking works
- where the service is available
- whether other pet owners trust it
- how to take the next step

RISKS_OF_SINGLE_PAGE
- less SEO opportunity for separate service pages
- less room for deeper trust-building
- harder to rank for multiple service-intent keywords over time
- navigation can feel shallow if business grows

BEST_USE_CASE
- fast launch
- ad landing page style website
- WhatsApp-first booking flow
- service businesses with 1 main offer and clear local market

SITE_ARCHITECTURE_OPTION_B

ARCHITECTURE_TYPE: MULTI_PAGE_SITE

When this is the right choice:
- the business wants stronger SEO
- dog and cat grooming need separate explanation
- service areas matter commercially
- the brand wants to feel more established
- multiple packages or grooming types exist
- trust and discoverability need to improve over time

PAGE_STRUCTURE

Page 01: Home
Purpose: Introduce the business, establish the value proposition, create immediate trust, and drive booking.
Sections:
- Hero
- Trust bar
- Services overview
- Benefits
- How it works
- Testimonials
- CTA band
- Footer

Page 02: Services
Purpose: Explain dog grooming, cat grooming, hygiene grooming, and package structures in more detail.
Sections:
- Hero
- Services
- Benefits
- Pricing
- FAQ
- CTA band
- Footer

Page 03: About
Purpose: Build emotional trust around the grooming philosophy, pet handling approach, and why home grooming is better.
Sections:
- Hero
- Problem-solution
- About-team
- Testimonials
- Footer

Page 04: Service Areas
Purpose: Clarify Bangalore neighborhoods or zones served and reduce friction for location-based users.
Sections:
- Hero
- Service-area
- FAQ
- CTA band
- Footer

Page 05: Contact / Book
Purpose: Provide the clearest booking path possible.
Sections:
- Hero
- Contact
- FAQ
- Footer

WHY_THIS_ARCHITECTURE_WORKS

This structure separates the major decision layers:
- Home = overall persuasion
- Services = offer clarity
- About = trust
- Service Areas = local relevance
- Contact = conversion

RISKS_OF_MULTI_PAGE
- more content required
- more design and dev effort
- if done poorly, can feel too big for a small business
- weak pages may reduce quality instead of improving it

BEST_USE_CASE
- serious local growth
- SEO-minded business owners
- businesses wanting more established positioning
- operators with time to maintain more content

HOMEPAGE_IDEATION_OPTION_1

DESIGN_DIRECTION: CALM_PREMIUM_CONVENIENCE

DESIGN_GOAL
- trustworthy
- gentle
- convenient
- emotionally safe for pets
- premium but approachable

VISUAL_CHARACTER
Mood: Calm, polished, warm, low-stress
Emotional Tone: Reassuring and pet-friendly
Density: Airy, not crowded
Scan Pattern: Easy to skim, large sections, soft card groupings

COLOR_DIRECTION
Primary Color Role: Soft blue or blue-green for trust and calm
Secondary Color Role: Warm neutral or pale cream for soft backgrounds
Accent Color Role: Warm coral or deeper teal for CTA emphasis

TYPOGRAPHY_DIRECTION
Heading Style: Rounded, friendly, clean, slightly premium
Body Style: Highly readable sans serif
Hierarchy Goal: Immediate comprehension, emotional softness, no harsh visual aggression

HERO_DIRECTION
Hero Layout: Split layout
Left side:
- strong headline
- clear supporting line
- primary booking CTA
- secondary “View services” action
- trust chips like “At-home service”, “Dogs & cats”, “Bangalore coverage”
Right side:
- image of calm pet grooming in a home environment
- optional floating cards with convenience signals

Hero Message Type:
Do not make the hero about grooming in general. Make it about stress-free at-home grooming for busy Bangalore pet owners.

MID_PAGE_FLOW
- immediate trust reassurance
- service explanation
- convenience and pet-comfort benefits
- process simplicity
- customer proof
- service area confirmation
- objections
- booking action

WHY_THIS_DESIGN_WORKS

This works because the business wins through emotional reassurance plus convenience. The design should not feel loud, flashy, or overly salesy. It should reduce anxiety — both for the pet owner and for the implied pet experience.

BEST_FOR
- pet care brands
- convenience-led local services
- emotionally sensitive services
- businesses wanting premium warmth

HOMEPAGE_IDEATION_OPTION_2

DESIGN_DIRECTION: DIRECT_HIGH_CONVERSION_LOCAL_SERVICE

DESIGN_GOAL
- immediately understandable
- fast to act on
- commercially sharp
- practical and trustworthy

VISUAL_CHARACTER
Mood: Clear, direct, efficient
Emotional Tone: Helpful, no-nonsense
Density: Slightly denser than premium calm version
Scan Pattern: Strong top-down conversion flow

COLOR_DIRECTION
Primary Color Role: Strong blue for trust and utility
Secondary Color Role: Light gray or very pale blue for surface variation
Accent Color Role: Bright but controlled contrasting color for CTA buttons

TYPOGRAPHY_DIRECTION
Heading Style: Bold, readable, action-oriented
Body Style: Utility-driven, clean
Hierarchy Goal: Fast comprehension and low bounce

HERO_DIRECTION
Hero Layout: Centered or left-weighted with strong CTA cluster
Hero Content Priorities:
- what service is offered
- where
- why it is easier than salon grooming
- booking button visible immediately

MID_PAGE_FLOW
- Hero
- Services snapshot
- Why home grooming is easier
- How it works
- Testimonials
- Areas served
- FAQ
- Final CTA

WHY_THIS_DESIGN_WORKS

This is less emotionally luxurious and more optimized for action. It works if the business prioritizes:
- leads
- mobile visitors
- quick scrollers
- local service demand capture

BEST_FOR
- lead-gen focus
- Google Ads landing pages
- practical local operators
- businesses prioritizing booking velocity

RECOMMENDED_FINAL_DIRECTION

Best Architecture Recommendation: Multi-page if the business wants long-term local growth and SEO. Single-page if speed and simplicity matter most right now.

Best Design Recommendation: Calm Premium Convenience if the brand wants trust, warmth, and better perceived quality. Direct High Conversion if the brand wants faster booking behavior and simpler execution.

RAG_READY_SUMMARY

Key Retrieval Concepts:
- local service business
- mobile pet grooming
- Bangalore
- single-page architecture
- multi-page architecture
- calm premium service design
- high-conversion local service design
- convenience-led website
- emotionally reassuring homepage
- trust plus action flow

## EXAMPLE_02

BUSINESS_TYPE: B2B SAAS

BUSINESS_NAME: CLINICFLOW AI

BUSINESS_PROFILE

Business Name: ClinicFlow AI
Business Type: SaaS / B2B software
Core Category: Clinic workflow automation software
Primary Geography: India-first, but potentially broader
Primary Offer Type: Software platform for appointment reminders, patient follow-ups, and intake automation
Business Maturity Assumption: Early-to-growth stage SaaS seeking demos or trials

Business Summary:
ClinicFlow AI helps small clinics automate patient communication and workflow tasks such as appointment reminders, follow-ups, and intake forms. It reduces no-shows, saves staff time, and improves process consistency. The business sells operational efficiency, reliability, and workflow simplification.

OFFER_STRUCTURE

Primary Product Modules:
- Appointment reminders
- WhatsApp follow-up automation
- Patient intake automation
- Missed appointment reduction
- Follow-up task automation
- Basic reporting or analytics dashboard

Offer Characteristics:
- Product is more complex than a local service
- Buyer may compare options before acting
- Trust, clarity, and feature explanation matter
- Buyers often need to understand workflow impact, not just features
- Value must be framed in business outcomes
- Product screenshots or UI mockups are very useful

Most Important Selling Angles:
- save staff time
- reduce no-shows
- automate repetitive communication
- improve clinic organization
- make operations feel smoother without hiring more staff

TARGET_AUDIENCE

Primary Audience: Clinic owners, practice managers, and small healthcare operators

Secondary Audience:
- front desk managers
- dental clinics
- physiotherapy clinics
- small therapy practices
- specialty clinics with operational bottlenecks

Audience Motivations:
- reduce manual work
- improve attendance rates
- avoid missed follow-ups
- create operational consistency
- modernize without major complexity

Likely Questions Before Demo or Trial:
- What exactly does the software do?
- Is it easy to set up?
- Will staff be able to use it?
- How much time does it save?
- Is this suitable for my clinic size?
- Does it work with WhatsApp?
- How much does it cost?
- What happens after onboarding?

BUYER_BEHAVIOR

Buying Journey Type: Mid-friction B2B evaluation with operational and trust considerations

User Intent Pattern:
The buyer usually wants to know:
- Is this relevant to my clinic?
- What problems does it solve?
- How does it work?
- What does the product look like?
- What results can I expect?
- Is it easy to adopt?
- What is pricing or demo process?

What Increases Conversion:
- clear pain-to-outcome framing
- product visuals
- strong feature hierarchy
- trust indicators
- use-case relevance
- operational outcome framing
- strong demo CTA
- pricing clarity or at least pricing logic

What Increases Bounce:
- vague AI-heavy language
- feature dumping without buyer outcomes
- unclear hero
- weak screenshots
- no explanation of workflow
- no proof or examples
- abstract product language

SITE_ARCHITECTURE_OPTION_A

ARCHITECTURE_TYPE: SINGLE_PAGE_SAAS_LANDING_PAGE

When this is the right choice:
- product is early-stage
- there is one focused offer
- primary goal is demo booking
- traffic comes from outreach or ads
- content depth can be concentrated on one page

PAGE_STRUCTURE

Page Count: 1

Page: Home

Suggested Section Order:
- Hero
- Trust bar
- Problem-solution
- Features
- Benefits
- How it works
- Results
- Pricing
- FAQ
- CTA band
- Footer

WHY_THIS_ARCHITECTURE_WORKS

A SaaS landing page can work well when the product has one tight promise. Everything can be sequenced on one page:
- pain
- solution
- feature proof
- process
- results
- pricing logic
- objection handling
- demo CTA

RISKS_OF_SINGLE_PAGE
- weaker SEO depth
- less room for specific use cases
- feature discovery may feel compressed
- revisiting evaluators may want more detailed pages

BEST_USE_CASE
- early-stage SaaS
- founders validating demand
- demo-booking funnel
- product with one narrow workflow outcome

SITE_ARCHITECTURE_OPTION_B

ARCHITECTURE_TYPE: MULTI_PAGE_B2B_SAAS_SITE

When this is the right choice:
- product has multiple modules
- use-case education matters
- buyers compare carefully
- SEO or content depth matters
- the business wants stronger perceived maturity

PAGE_STRUCTURE

Page 01: Home
Purpose: Position the product clearly, frame the pain solved, show the product, and drive demos.
Sections:
- Hero
- Trust bar
- Problem-solution
- Features overview
- Results
- CTA band
- Footer

Page 02: Features
Purpose: Explain product modules in operational detail.
Sections:
- Hero
- Features
- Benefits
- How it works
- FAQ
- CTA band
- Footer

Page 03: Use Cases
Purpose: Show relevance by clinic type or workflow scenario.
Sections:
- Hero
- Problem-solution
- Benefits
- Results
- CTA band
- Footer

Page 04: Pricing
Purpose: Show plan structure, fit by clinic size, and remove cost ambiguity.
Sections:
- Hero
- Pricing
- FAQ
- CTA band
- Footer

Page 05: Contact / Book Demo
Purpose: Convert interested users into scheduled demos.
Sections:
- Hero
- Contact
- FAQ
- Footer

WHY_THIS_ARCHITECTURE_WORKS

This structure mirrors a B2B buying journey:
- Home = positioning
- Features = understanding
- Use Cases = relevance
- Pricing = evaluation
- Demo page = action

RISKS_OF_MULTI_PAGE
- more copywriting required
- more design and product visuals needed
- more internal consistency required
- can feel too large if product is still vague

BEST_USE_CASE
- growing SaaS
- B2B evaluation cycles
- feature-rich software
- SEO and demo-generation together

HOMEPAGE_IDEATION_OPTION_1

DESIGN_DIRECTION: CRISP_OPERATIONAL_B2B_CLARITY

DESIGN_GOAL
- reliable
- easy to understand
- operationally useful
- low-friction
- serious but modern

VISUAL_CHARACTER
Mood: Clear, structured, efficient
Emotional Tone: Competent and practical
Density: Moderate, information-rich but controlled
Scan Pattern: Fast above-the-fold comprehension, then structured proof

COLOR_DIRECTION
Primary Color Role: Blue or blue-green for reliability and software seriousness
Secondary Color Role: Light neutral surfaces for dashboards/cards
Accent Color Role: Strong CTA emphasis color, used selectively

TYPOGRAPHY_DIRECTION
Heading Style: Sharp, modern, B2B-safe
Body Style: Highly readable, compact, efficient
Hierarchy Goal: Buyers should instantly understand product category, problem, and action

HERO_DIRECTION
Hero Layout: Split hero with product UI mockup
Left side:
- pain + promise headline
- subheading focused on workflow improvement
- primary demo CTA
- optional secondary CTA like “See features”
Right side:
- dashboard or workflow screenshot
- floating mini cards showing reminders, no-show reduction, or patient follow-up examples

Hero Message Type:
Not “AI for healthcare.” Instead: Automate reminders and follow-ups for small clinics without adding more admin work.

MID_PAGE_FLOW
- hero clarity
- trust strip or workflow credibility
- core pain-to-solution section
- feature blocks tied to outcomes
- how it works
- results / efficiency proof
- pricing or demo CTA
- FAQ
- closing CTA

WHY_THIS_DESIGN_WORKS

The buyer is operational and outcome-driven. The design should feel:
- easy to trust
- easy to scan
- easy to map to clinic workflows

BEST_FOR
- practical B2B buyers
- clinic managers
- operations-heavy software
- demo-first acquisition

HOMEPAGE_IDEATION_OPTION_2

DESIGN_DIRECTION: MODERN_AI_NATIVE_SAAS

DESIGN_GOAL
- advanced
- modern
- startup-grade
- intelligent
- differentiated from old clinic software

VISUAL_CHARACTER
Mood: Contemporary, layered, high-signal
Emotional Tone: Capable and innovative
Density: Moderate to high, but visually organized
Scan Pattern: Bold hero, then modular product storytelling

COLOR_DIRECTION
Primary Color Role: Deeper tech-forward brand color
Secondary Color Role: Soft gradient or muted surfaces
Accent Color Role: Bright focused CTA highlight

TYPOGRAPHY_DIRECTION
Heading Style: Strong, modern, slightly more dynamic
Body Style: Clean and product-led
Hierarchy Goal: Create excitement without reducing clarity

HERO_DIRECTION
Hero Layout: Product-first hero with layered UI + workflow cards
Left side:
- stronger, more ambitious headline
- short product promise
- CTA
- concise proof chips
Right side:
- layered dashboard visual
- chat / reminder / automation cards
- subtle gradient background

MID_PAGE_FLOW
- bold hero
- workflow automation proof
- feature stack
- outcomes
- use-case modules
- pricing or demo CTA
- FAQ
- final CTA

WHY_THIS_DESIGN_WORKS

This design is useful if the product wants to feel clearly newer and smarter than traditional clinic software. However, it must remain concrete. If visual modernity overtakes clarity, conversion may drop.

BEST_FOR
- AI-positioned SaaS
- investor-facing perception
- startup-grade brand ambitions
- products needing differentiation from legacy tools

RECOMMENDED_FINAL_DIRECTION

Best Architecture Recommendation: Single-page if the business is still validating product-market fit or mainly running demo campaigns. Multi-page if the product has multiple modules and buyers need more evaluation depth.

Best Design Recommendation: Crisp Operational B2B Clarity if the audience is practical and operations-led. Modern AI-Native SaaS if the business also wants stronger innovation signaling.

RAG_READY_SUMMARY

Key Retrieval Concepts:
- b2b saas
- clinic workflow software
- single-page saas architecture
- multi-page saas architecture
- demo-first homepage
- operational software design
- product-led hero
- product screenshot hero
- trust and workflow clarity
- AI-native SaaS design
